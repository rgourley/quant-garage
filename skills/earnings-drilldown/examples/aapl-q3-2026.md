## What this output reveals about the AAPL setup

- **Realized has compressed materially.** ±1.6% over 8q is dramatically
  lower than AAPL's ±4-5% historical norm from 2018-2021. Post-COVID,
  post-services-mix-up, post-buyback-machine: the company prints into
  a saturated coverage tape with no surprise tolerance and no surprise
  delivery. Implied move stayed elevated because vol surfaces price in
  the tail, but the realized has caught up to the new normal.
- **Straddle is expensive in pp terms, not just ratio terms.** 4.2pp of
  premium over realized is real money for vol sellers and a real cost
  for premium buyers. The "263% rich" framing (mispricing_pct) is
  technically correct but visually loud; the pp framing is what an
  options trader would actually quote.
- **PEAD is weak.** AAPL is mega-cap with saturated coverage. t-stat
  0.33 says don't bet on a post-print drift. Hold or trim on
  conviction, not on PEAD pattern.
- **Beat streak is long but surprise size is modest.** 8/8 is great
  for sentiment but the average 4.7% surprise is the real signal:
  management sandbags, the market knows, and the first miss when it
  comes will be violent.
- **Peer reaction is muted.** TSM at 0.58 beta is the highest, and the
  curated mega-cap basket averaged +1.1% on AAPL beats. AAPL prints
  don't move the basket as much as semi prints move semis.

## What a UI builder does with this

Consume the JSON. Render `implied_vs_realized` as a comparison chart
(implied bar vs realized bar with a "rich/cheap" indicator). Render
`print_history` as a beat/miss heatmap with reaction sparkline. Render
`post_earnings_drift` as a small bar chart with significance shading.
Render `peer_reaction` as a table of peers ranked by beta. Catalysts
go in a side panel. Sources link from each datapoint for audit trail.

The note format above is one default. The JSON is the contract.
